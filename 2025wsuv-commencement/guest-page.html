For Guests

Congratulations, Class of 2025!

Attend WSU Vancouver’s 2025 Commencement Ceremony

1-3 p.m. Saturday, May 3

Where: Cascades Amphitheater
17200 NE Delfel Road, Ridgefield, WA 98642

The commencement ceremony is free and open to all. Tickets are not required. There is no guest limit. Parking is free.

Who Can Participate

WSU students from any campus who have applied for their degree or have already received it can register to participate.

Guest Parking

Parking is free and available onsite. The venue has a large parking lot that accommodates more than 7,000 vehicles. Plan to arrive early because traffic, security checks and the walk to the venue entrance can take time.

Amphitheater parking guide

Security and venue policies

All guests will go through security screening upon entry. Expect metal detectors and bag checks.

What to bring

  • Small clutches, wristlets or fanny packs (4.5” x 6.5” or smaller)
  • Clear plastic bags (up to 12” x 12” x 6”)

Accordion/expandable element: What to leave at home

  • Large bags, coolers, outside food or drinks
  • Balloons, gift bags, fireworks
  • Weapons (including pocketknives)
  • Umbrellas, strollers, playpens
  • Pets (service animals permitted)

For full venue policies, visit the Amphitheater Rules webpage.

Parking and mobility assistance

We want all guests to have an enjoyable experience.

  • Accessible parking is located just outside the East Plaza. A visible state-issued accessible placard is required to park in this lot.
  • Accessible parking is limited, so arriving early is recommended.
  • Turn on emergency flashers to help the venue's parking staff identify your needs. They will direct vehicles to the appropriate lot.
  • The pathway to the east accessible entrance is paved.
  • If you are dropping someone off with access needs, let the parking attendants know and they will let you drop off at the front of the accessible entrance.

Services for Deaf or Hard of Hearing Guests

  • To download an assistive listening app onto your cell phone, visit a guest services tent located near the accessible entrance and the main gates. You will need to bring your own headphones to use the app.
  • Sign-language interpreters will be positioned in front of section 101, and some seats will be set aside for guests requesting ASL interpreting. Seating will be on a first-come, first-served basis. Please limit these seats to guests using ASL interpreting and one attendant.
  • CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation) will be available for the ceremony.

Seating and accommodations

  • The amphitheater has accessible seating according to industry standards, including seating for people using wheelchairs.
  • Because accessible seating is limited, please use it only for people with disabilities and one additional guest each, so that space remains available for all people in need.
  • Ceremony viewing will not be available from the lawn seating area.

Medication needs

  • If you need to take prescribed medication during the event, the medication must be in its original container with a name that is verifiable with a photo ID.
  • If you do not have a container with a name on it, the venue staff may request documentation to confirm the need for the medication.
  • Over-the-counter medication must be in its original packaging.
  • Only bring what is required for the duration of the event.

    • For a complete list of venue accessibility information, visit the Cascades Amphitheater Accessibility Information webpage.

Helpful Tips for Guests

Arrive early and plan ahead
Traffic, parking and security screenings take time. The venue has a large lot, but the walk to the entrance is long. The ceremony starts promptly at 1 p.m.

Pack light
Only small bags are allowed. See security guidelines above for details.

Dress for the weather
Commencement takes place rain or shine. Be prepared for any weather conditions.

Wear comfortable shoes
Expect a mix of gravel, pavement, ramps and stairs.

Follow venue rules
Aisles must stay clear for safety. If you want to sit with your group, arrive together.

Concessions are available
Bring credit, debit or mobile pay
Food and drinks will be sold at the venue.
