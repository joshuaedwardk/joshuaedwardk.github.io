Common Questions and FAQ's

What’s the difference between graduation and commencement?

Graduation refers to the completion of your academic requirements and the formal awarding of your degree. Commencement is a celebratory ceremony held in May to recognize and honor students completing academic programs.

Who is eligible to participate in commencement?

If you graduated in December 2024, you are eligible to participate.

If you are graduating in May 2025, you must apply by March 7 to be included in the program and ceremony.

Summer graduates: If you are graduating in July 2025, apply to graduate by March 7 to be included in the ceremony. If you are not participating in the ceremony, you have until May 24 to apply to graduate.

Will there be a professional photographer on site?

Yes. GradImages will take professional photos during the ceremony at three points:

  • Before you walk across the stage
  • As you receive your diploma cover
  • At the bottom of the ramp before returning to your seat

Graduates will provide their contact information on the back of their name card so GradImages can send photo proofs after the event. Proofs will be available by late May or early June.

Will the ceremony be livestreamed?

Yes. Watch the livestream online.

Is transportation provided for graduates?

No. Graduates and guests must arrange their own transportation and parking.

How long is the ceremony?

The ceremony itself lasts about two hours. Expect additional time for arrival and check-in. After the ceremony, it will take time to find your family and friends, make your way to your car and exit the parking area.

Will the event take place outside?

The ceremony will take place inside the covered section of the Cascades Amphitheater. However, the graduate line-up areas and greeting areas for guests are outdoors, so be prepared for that. In the event of inclement weather, the ceremony will take place as scheduled, and seating will remain in the covered area.

Will I receive my diploma at commencement?

No, not at the ceremony. Degrees are conferred after final grades are posted, which takes time. WSU needs to verify that all degree requirements are met, and this process can take a few weeks. Once finalized, your diploma will be mailed to the address listed in myWSU, so make sure your information is up to date. You can check myWSU to see if your degree has posted.

What should you expect during the ceremony?

The ceremony follows a structured order, including the processional, speeches, the awarding of degrees and the recessional. Marshals will direct graduates throughout the event.
