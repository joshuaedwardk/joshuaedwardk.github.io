Regalia and Attire

Traditional caps and gowns are required at all WSU in-person commencement ceremonies.

All regalia is ordered through Herff Jones. The Registrar’s Office and The Bookie are not involved in regalia orders—please contact Herff Jones directly for any issues.

  • Order by Feb. 14 to pick up at The Bookie (free shipping).
  • Pick up dates: 10 a.m. – 4 p.m. Monday – Friday, March 17 – May 9

Returning regalia

Regalia rentals must be returned by May 10. Orders shipped to your home will include a prepaid return label and instructions.

Regalia picked up from The Bookie must be returned by 3 p.m. May 9.

Regalia Guidelines and Tips

These guidelines apply to the day of the commencement ceremony.

  • Tassels: Undergraduates wear their tassels on the right side and move them to the left as part of the ceremony. Master’s and doctoral candidates wear their tassels on the left.
  • Stoles, sashes and cords: Wear underneath the WSU yoke.

Honor Cords

Honor cords are reserved for undergraduate students who have earned a 3.5 or higher GPA and have at least 30 graded WSU credits. Eligible students will receive a custom WSU honor cord in crimson and silver.

  • Cum Laude (3.50 – 3.69)
  • Magna Cum Laude (3.70 – 3.89)
  • Summa Cum Laude (3.90 or higher)

Honor cords are distributed at rehearsal and commencement.

Commencement honors are based on fall semester GPA and credits, while final honors (on diplomas and transcripts) reflect all WSU-graded coursework.

Stoles

First-generation graduates: WSU provides a first-generation stole, which will be distributed at rehearsal and commencement.

Additional cords and stoles may be worn in accordance with university guidelines.

Frequently asked questions

What should I wear underneath my gown?

Wear comfortable shoes, as you will walk and stand on various surfaces. Business casual or business professional attire is encouraged. Choose clothing with at least one pocket for essentials like keys, wallet or phone—storage is not available at the venue.

Can I decorate my cap?

Yes, but decorations should be appropriate and must not extend beyond the edges of the cap.

Is regalia required to participate in Commencement?

Yes, regalia is required for all graduates attending the ceremony.

Can I keep my cap and gown?

You may keep your cap and tassel. Rented gowns and hoods must be returned.

Regalia Guidelines and Tips

These guidelines will help you prepare your regalia and know what to expect during the ceremony.

  • Tassels: Undergraduates wear their tassels on the right side and move them to the left as part of the ceremony. Master’s and doctoral candidates wear their tassels on the left.

  • Stoles, sashes and cords: Wear underneath the crimson and gray WSU yoke.

  • Undergraduates: Wear a black cap and gown with the WSU yoke. See how to wear your cap and gown. Ceremony volunteers will help adjust your regalia.

  • Master’s degree candidates: Arrive wearing your academic hood. Watch how to put it on. Volunteers will be in the gathering area to help with adjustments.

  • Doctoral candidates: Carry your hood over one arm during the processional. You will be hooded on stage as part of the ceremony. Watch how to carry your hood and what to expect during the hooding ceremony.
